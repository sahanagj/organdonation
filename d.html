SAVE LIFE
BE AN ORGAN DONOR

Organ donation is giving an organ to help someone who needs a transplant. Transplants can save or greatly enhance the lives of other people. But this relies on donors and their families agreeing to donate their organ.


HOW IT WORKS?

1. Register as donor

The process of donation most often begins with your consent to be a donor by registering. Signing up does not guarantee you will be able to donate your organs, eyes, or tissuesâ€”and registering usually takes place many years before donation becomes possible. But it is the first step to being eligible to save lives.

2. Medical care

For someone to become a deceased donor, he or she has to die in very specific circumstances.The medical team does everything possible to save the patient's life. At this point, whether or not the person is a registered donor is not considered.

3. Brain death testing

If the patient is dead and is not responding, physicians will perform a series of tests to determine if brain death has occurred. Brain death is death and it is irreversible. Only after brain death has been confirmed and the time of death noted, can organ donation become a possibility.


4. Authorizing donation

See if the deceased is registered as a donor.If so, that will serve as legal consent for donation.After authorization, a medical evaluation takes place, including obtaining the deceased's complete medical and social history from the family.

5. Recovery & transporting organs

While the search for matching recipients is under way, the deceased donor's organs are maintained on artificial support.The surgical team removes the organs and tissues from the donor's body in an operating room. All incisions are surgically closed. Organ donation does not interfere with open-casket funerals.

6. Transplantation

The transplant operation takes place after the transport team arrives at the hospital with the new organ.Surgical teams work around the clock as needed to transplant the new organs into the waiting recipients.
